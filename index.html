Biography

Usher Raymond IV, born on October 14, 1978, is a prominent American singer, songwriter, and dancer. He burst onto the music scene at the young age of 16 with his self-titled debut album in 1994. However, it was his second album, "My Way" (1997), that propelled him to fame, featuring hits like "Nice & Slow," "You Make Me Wanna...," and "My Way." Throughout his career, Usher has consistently delivered chart-topping albums and singles. His third album, "8701" (2001), produced hits like "U Remind Me" and "U Got It Bad," earning him his first Grammy Awards. "Confessions" (2004) solidified his status as a top-selling artist, with singles such as "Yeah!," "Burn," and "Confessions Part II." Usher's success continued with albums like "Here I Stand" (2008) and "Raymond v. Raymond" (2010), both of which debuted at number one on the Billboard 200 chart. He has also ventured into acting, appearing in films like "The Faculty" (1998) and "In The Mix" (2005), as well as television shows like "Moesha" and "The Voice." Beyond his musical and acting accomplishments, Usher is known for his philanthropic efforts. In 1999, he founded the non-profit organization Usher's New Look, which aims to provide young people with education and real-world experience. He has also been involved in various humanitarian causes and received recognition for his contributions. Overall, Usher's impact on music and popular culture is undeniable. With numerous awards, accolades, and record-breaking achievements, he has solidified his place as one of the best-selling music artists of all time and an influential figure in contemporary R&B and pop music.

Early Life

Usher Raymond IV was born on October 14, 1978, in Dallas, Texas, to Jonnetta Patton (née O'Neal) from Tennessee, and Usher Raymond III. Much of his early life was spent in Chattanooga, Tennessee, where he was raised by his mother, stepfather, and half-brother James Lackey, following his father's departure when Usher was just a year old. Usher's musical talents were first recognized in his local church youth choir at the age of nine, where his grandmother noticed his singing ability. It wasn't until he joined a singing group, however, that his family began to see his potential for a professional career in music. Recognizing the need for a larger platform to showcase his talents, Usher's family relocated to Atlanta, Georgia, where there were more opportunities for aspiring singers. In Atlanta, Usher attended North Springs High School, where he continued to hone his musical skills and pursue his passion for singing. This move to Atlanta proved to be a crucial step in Usher's journey toward becoming a successful recording artist.

Musical style and influences

Usher's music spans various genres, primarily R&B but also incorporating elements of pop and other styles. His ability to adapt to different musical trends has been recognized, with Rolling Stone noting his skill in navigating pop's evolution, from crunk to EDM. Complex praised his ability to blend pop sensibilities with R&B authenticity, earning him a reputation as an "R&B Casanova" with autobiographical storytelling focused on lifestyle, relationships, and love. Throughout his career, Usher's sound has evolved, drawing inspiration from a diverse range of influences. His early work showed influences of new jack swing and soul, while later albums like "Confessions" showcased top-tier pop-soul production. "Here I Stand" leaned towards ballads, drawing comparisons to Stevie Wonder, while "Raymond v Raymond" fused R&B, hip-hop, and pop seamlessly. "Looking 4 Myself" marked a departure into progressive R&B, exploring hip-hop ballads, synth-pop, and EDM influences. His album "Hard II Love" continued this trend, pushing the boundaries of R&B with brooding atmospherics and a dynamic incorporation of various musical styles. Usher has cited Michael Jackson as his biggest influence, both musically and personally, praising his humanitarianism and cultural impact. Prince has also been a significant influence, inspiring Usher's expansive approach to music and genre-blending. Other influences include Marvin Gaye, Stevie Wonder, Luther Vandross, and Boyz II Men, among others.

Performance

Usher's stage presence has earned praise for his high-energy choreography and captivating showmanship. Critics have lauded his ability to command the stage solo, comparing him to iconic performers like James Brown and Michael Jackson. His performances, described as packed with charisma, have been noted for their seamless blend of singing and dancing. Usher's influence from his childhood idols is evident, with his performances characterized by a mix of old-school showmanship and contemporary flair. He's been recognized for his skillful execution of intricate choreography and his dynamic vocal range, often punctuated by exuberant vocal flourishes. Additionally, his versatility as a musician extends to playing instruments like the piano, drums, and bass guitar, adding depth to his live shows. With a talented backing band and vocalists, Usher's live performances are celebrated for their masterful execution and electrifying energy, making him a standout presence in the realm of R&B and pop music.

Musical style and influences

  • Music

    Usher founded US Records in 2002, a subsidiary of Clive Davis's J Records. The label released the soundtrack to "In the Mix" in 2005, featuring acts like Justin Bieber and One Chance. He also co-founded the Raymond Braun Media Group with Scooter Braun, managing Justin Bieber. In 2023, Usher co-founded Mega with L.A. Reid and Larry Jackson. Additionally, he served as a mentor on American Idol and as a coach on The Voice, winning with contestant Josh Kaufman in 2014.

  • Products and services

    Usher's entrepreneurial ventures extend beyond music into various industries, showcasing his versatility and business acumen. He has delved into the fragrance market with several successful lines, including "Usher He" and "Usher She," as well as "UR for Men" and "UR for Women." Additionally, he's ventured into the realm of music streaming as a co-owner of Tidal, a service focused on high-quality audio and video content, alongside other artists like Jay-Z, Beyoncé, and Rihanna. Usher has also partnered with Yoobi, a school supplies company, to create a line of products aimed at supporting education and providing resources to underserved communities. Through these ventures, Usher demonstrates a commitment to giving back and empowering future generations, aligning with his philanthropic efforts through the New Look Foundation.

  • Endorsements

    Usher's collaborations with major brands have extended his influence beyond music into the realms of fashion, gaming, technology, and more. Partnerships with companies like MasterCard, Belvedere Vodka, Microsoft, Samsung, Pepsi, and Rémy Martin have allowed him to engage with fans in innovative ways while also supporting charitable causes. Whether through the creation of a sleek Usher Debit MasterCard or lending his choreography to Microsoft's Dance Central 3 game, Usher has consistently leveraged his brand to create memorable experiences for his audience. His involvement in advertising campaigns and initiatives like MasterCard's Priceless Surprises and Pepsi's New Pepsi Challenge demonstrates his commitment to engaging with fans and promoting social responsibility. Usher's collaborations with brands continue to showcase his versatility and entrepreneurial spirit, cementing his status as a multifaceted entertainer and influencer.

  • Philanthropy

    Usher's philanthropic endeavors have left a lasting impact, with his New Look Foundation aiming to empower youth through education and real-world experiences. Through initiatives like Camp New Look and Our Block, he has revitalized communities and supported education from 8th grade through post-secondary education, boasting a 100% graduation rate among participants. Beyond his foundation, Usher has been involved in various charitable efforts, including benefit concerts for Hurricane Katrina relief and campaigns promoting civic engagement. In the wake of Hurricane Katrina, his Project Restart initiative provided housing assistance to hundreds of families in New Orleans. His dedication earned him the key to the city and his own proclaimed day. Usher's commitment to social causes has been recognized with honors such as the Freedom Award from the National Civil Rights Museum. He continues to give back, donating to causes like historically black colleges and universities and contributing to Colin Kaepernick's charity campaign. These efforts have solidified Usher's legacy as not only a musical icon but also a humanitarian.

  • Politics and activism

    Usher has been actively involved in political and social activism throughout his career. In 2007, he launched the "Change in the South" campaign in support of Barack Obama's presidential bid and served as the National Co-chair for Obama's "Vote for Change" voter registration campaign. He continued to support Obama's reelection campaign in 2012. Usher's activism extends beyond politics, as seen in his partnership with organizations like Sankofa, focusing on issues of social injustice. He has hosted events and discussions alongside prominent figures like Bill Clinton and Harry Belafonte to address these issues and raise awareness through initiatives like his music video "Chains." Additionally, Usher has participated in fundraisers for political candidates like Hillary Clinton and was selected as a delegate for the Presidential Committee for Arts and Humanities, seeking to strengthen cultural ties between the United States and Cuba. Through his music and advocacy, Usher remains dedicated to effecting positive change in society.

  • Legacy

    Usher's influence in the music industry is undeniable, with Billboard recognizing him as the Hot 100 Artist of the 2000s for his remarkable success and chart-topping hits. His album "Confessions" not only achieved commercial success by selling 20 million copies worldwide but also left an indelible mark on pop culture, earning comparisons to Michael Jackson's "Thriller" as a defining album of its era. Renowned for his exceptional dancing skills, Usher has been hailed as the best dancer in pop since Michael Jackson by VH1 and The Guardian. His impact transcends his music, as demonstrated by James Brown's declaration of him as the Godson of Soul at the Grammy Awards in 2005. Usher's influence extends to a multitude of contemporary artists, including Chris Brown, Trey Songz, Drake, and Justin Bieber, who credit him as a major inspiration for their own careers. His legacy continues to shape the landscape of pop music, earning him accolades such as the BET Honors Musical Arts award in 2015 for his transformative contributions to the industry.

  • Achievements

    Usher's illustrious career spans multiple decades, marked by a plethora of awards and record-breaking achievements. With 8 Grammy Awards and induction into prestigious halls of fame like the Georgia Music Hall of Fame and the Hollywood Walk of Fame, Usher's impact on music is undeniable. His album "Confessions" achieved Diamond certification, solidifying its status as a monumental success in the industry. Usher's chart-topping singles, including "OMG," showcase his enduring popularity and influence across generations. He holds the record for spending the most weeks at number one on the Billboard Hot 100, a testament to his enduring appeal. Additionally, Usher's presence on various all-time lists, such as Billboard's "Greatest of All Time Hot 100 Artists" and VH1's "Greatest Songs of the 2000s," cements his legacy as one of the most significant figures in contemporary music.
